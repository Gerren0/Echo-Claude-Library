## 1. Brand Strategy

*[Full page: echo-brand-guidelines.webflow.io/brand-strategy]*

### Brand Positioning

For shippers and carriers, Echo positions itself as the 3PL that solves transportation needs through three core strengths:

- **Expert Partnership** — Acts as an extension of the client's team, with 24/7 support from industry experts and relationships built on trust.
- **Innovative Technology** — Uses AI, machine learning, and advanced load-matching algorithms, plus API/EDI capabilities, without requiring clients to change how they operate.
- **Reliable Carrier Network** — A large, dependable network across multiple transportation modes that keeps supply chains running.

### Mission Statement

> Build the most innovative and reliable technology-driven transportation network, leveraging data to efficiently connect shippers with capacity.

*The mission defines why Echo exists; adhering to it enables the vision.*

### Vision Statement

*(Vision statement content was not populated on the live page — placeholder only.)*

*The vision defines the impact Echo strives to create for its audiences.*

### Brand Personality

Four traits ensure the brand feels human rather than cold or impersonal:

| Trait | Description |
|---|---|
| **Trustworthy** | A dependable partner that prepares for the unexpected and carries the load alongside clients. |
| **Innovative** | Believes technology enables growth; leverages AI, machine learning, and load-matching algorithms. |
| **Proactive** | Works to resolve issues before they arise, keeping clients informed and confident. |
| **Passionate** | Deeply invested in client success, paired with 24/7 expert support. |

### Brand Promise

> Solve any transportation need.

*The single takeaway Echo wants every audience to retain.*

### How to Describe Echo

| Context | Description |
|---|---|
| **In a press release** | Echo simplifies transportation management for carriers and shippers, solving any transportation need through expert partnership, innovative technology, and a reliable carrier network. |
| **In an elevator** | Echo connects businesses that need to ship products with carriers who transport goods quickly, securely, and cost-effectively. |
| **In a cab** | "I work for a transportation management company that connects carriers and shippers." |

## 2. Messaging Frameworks

*[Full page: echo-brand-guidelines.webflow.io/messaging-frameworks]*

### Brand Pillars

Three benefit categories anchor all audience messaging and content strategy:

1. **Expert partners by your side** — Strategic, trust-based partnerships with real-time human support; Echo acts as an extension of the client's team.
2. **Innovative technology at your fingertips** — Advanced analytics and load-matching algorithms provide visibility and predictability; scalable via tools, API/EDI, or phone support.
3. **Reliable access you need** — A large carrier network connects shippers with capacity and carriers with freight, keeping supply chains and capacity fully utilized.

---

### Audience Persona: Carriers

Carriers match capacity with loads, dispatch drivers efficiently, and oversee driver treatment.

- **Challenge:** Unreliable/inefficient communication from 3PLs and time lost to inefficient processes.
- **Goal:** Long-lasting 3PL partnerships and full capacity utilization.
- **Solution:** 24/7 expert support plus freight that keeps drivers moving.

**Value Proposition:** Reliable partnership and access to freight.

#### Carrier Key Messages

**Pillar 1 — Expert partners by your side**

| Key Message | Proof Points |
|---|---|
| *Partnerships that go the distance.* Recurring freight relationships, loyalty benefits, and status-based rewards, backed by dedicated relationship management. | Echo received an ISO Service Award in 2021; dedicated relationship management; rewards program tied to carrier status. |
| *Keeping you up to date on what matters most.* Proactive, frequent communication and 24/7 support to resolve issues immediately. | Carriers cite wasted time during load pickups; Echo offers insourced, U.S.-based 24/7/365 customer service and dispatch. |
| *World-class support from the people behind the tech.* Human expert support paired with technology. | Carriers often can't find a human to help with tech issues; Echo employs Truckload, LTL, Partial, and Intermodal subject matter experts. |

**Pillar 2 — Innovative technology at your fingertips**

| Key Message | Proof Points |
|---|---|
| *Delivering unparalleled, tech-enabled results.* Advanced analytics and load-matching algorithms combined with expert staff. | Proprietary technology built in-house; dedicated AI/ML analytics team. |
| *Diversified technology solutions that meet your needs.* Scalable options based on each carrier's existing tech maturity. | Carriers with in-house tech get full API/EDI integration; carriers without it use EchoDrive. |
| *A marketplace of opportunity.* EchoDrive lets carriers search, bid, and book freight via web or mobile. | Dispatchers use the EchoDrive web portal, drivers use the mobile app; carriers can search, submit offers, and book online. |

**Pillar 3 — Reliable access you need**

| Key Message | Proof Points |
|---|---|
| *Freight that keeps you moving.* Reliable freight access keeps capacity fully utilized and connects loads efficiently. | Carriers want full utilization and efficient load-connecting; EchoDrive provides efficient freight access. |
| *Providing load transparency.* Careful matching of freight to capacity (size/weight) minimizes delays. | Automated matching using internal and external data; Echo can handle shipments of any shape or size. |
| *Grow your business with Echo.* Access to an expansive shipper network across North America. | Echo is one of the largest 3PLs in North America; helps carriers balance networks and maximize utilization. |

### Audience Persona: Shippers

Shippers oversee import/export of shipments, timely delivery, customer expectations, damage/claims reduction, planning, and inventory control.

- **Challenge:** Managing the trade-off between service and cost.
- **Goal:** Hit on-time delivery goals through reliable capacity access.
- **Solution:** A reliable carrier network plus technology offering shipment visibility.

**Value Proposition:** Reliable access to capacity and best-in-class service.

## 3. Verbal Expression

*[Full page: echo-brand-guidelines.webflow.io/verbal-expression]*

### Brand Story

Echo exists to bring clarity and reliability to the historically unpredictable transportation industry. Partnership sits at the center of the brand — relationships built on technology and trust — with Echo acting as an extension of the client's team to help carry the load and drive their business forward.

### Our Voice and Tone

Voice is expressed as a set of "do this / not that" pairs:

| Do | Avoid |
|---|---|
| **Agile** — Goal-oriented, tech-enabled, solution-focused messaging. | **Corporate** — Jargon and overly technical language that feels impersonal. |
| **Passionate** — Confident, proactive, forward-thinking. | **Blunt** — Overly frank or matter-of-fact. |
| **Genuine** — Transparent, clear, free of hidden agendas. | **Casual** — Colloquialisms or slang that undercut expertise. |

### Voice and Tone Spectrum

Tone shifts along a formal-to-organic spectrum depending on channel and audience intent:

| Channel | Example | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Educational Content** (white papers, reports) | Headline: "Optimizing Performance Metrics to Improve Transportation Management" | Professional tone with specific, data-driven proof points to build credibility. |
| **Website & Marketing Communications** | Headline: "Innovative Tech Driving Your Business Forward" | Forward-thinking, confident tone reflecting innovation and proactivity. |
| **Organic & Paid Social Media** | "We've got your back" / "We carry the load alongside you" | Fun, conversational — the most informal channel, showing personality. |
| **Internal Communication** | "Kudos to the team for hitting this month's sales goals" | Relatable, culture-building tone that maintains morale and collaboration. |

### AP Style Preferences

Echo follows the **Associated Press Stylebook** as its base style authority, with these additional house rules:

| Rule | Guidance | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Headlines** | Main headlines in title case; all others in sentence case. | "Your Strategic Partner for Any Shipping Need" (title case) |
| **Numerals** | Spell out one–nine (except "3PL"); use numerals for 10+. | "A customer is tracking four shipments." / "A customer is tracking 11 shipments." / "Echo is a 3PL." |
| **Percentages** | Numeral + % symbol, unless starting a sentence. | "Echo requires 99% of shipments to be exception-free." / "Ninety-nine percent of shipments must be exception free." |
| **Serial comma** | Always use the Oxford comma. | "...dispatchers, owner-operators, and drivers." |
| **And vs. ampersand** | Either "and" or "&" acceptable in headlines or body copy. | "Echo Global Logistics Named a 2023 Top 3PL & Cold Storage Provider by Food Logistics" |
| **Em dash (—)** | Use with spaces on either side, like a parenthetical. | "Select a mode and tell us your shipping origin and destination — we'll provide an instant quote." |

## 4. Logo

*[Full page: echo-brand-guidelines.webflow.io/logo]*

### Primary Logo
The wordmark paired with the Echo Range Lines. As the most frequently viewed brand asset, it must be applied consistently — never stretched, recreated, distorted, or altered. Available in Primary, Reverse, Black, and White versions. [Download](https://www.echo.com/wp-content/themes/ws/brand-assets/01-Echo-Logo.zip)

### Primary Logo Badge
The Echo "E" plus Range Lines, used when the primary logo isn't feasible (size constraints) or would be repetitive (e.g., paginated documents, slide decks). Never the first brand touchpoint. Available in Primary, Reverse, Black, and White. [Download](https://www.echo.com/wp-content/themes/ws/brand-assets/01-Echo-Icon.zip)

### Echo® Logo With Tagline
Reserved for spotlight moments where the logo is the sole visual element — advertising, sales/marketing materials, signage, tradeshow installs, vehicles, PR, and promotional items. Use except where the tagline would be too small to read. Available in Primary and Reverse. [Download](https://www.echo.com/wp-content/themes/ws/brand-assets/01-Echo-Logo-Tagline.zip)

### EchoDrive® Logo
Wordmark plus product badge; used consistently across EchoDrive-applicable collateral only. [Download](https://www.echo.com/wp-content/themes/ws/brand-assets/03-EchoDrive-Logo.zip)

### EchoShip® Logo
Wordmark plus product badge; same consistency rules apply. [Download](https://www.echo.com/wp-content/themes/ws/brand-assets/02-EchoShip-Logo.zip)

### EchoTrak Logo
Wordmark plus product badge; same consistency rules apply. [Download](https://www.echo.com/wp-content/themes/ws/brand-assets/04-EchoTrak-Logo.zip)

### Components
- **Echo Logo** = wordmark + "Echo impact lines"
- **EchoDrive Logo** = EchoDrive wordmark + custom badge
- **EchoShip Logo** = EchoShip wordmark + custom badge
- **EchoTrak Logo** = EchoTrak wordmark + custom badge

Logo spacing, alignment, scale, and shape must never be altered — use artwork exactly as provided.

### Clearspace
For all logo/badge variants (Echo, EchoDrive, EchoShip, EchoTrak): clear space equals the height of the Echo "E" in the wordmark; for the badge alone, clear space is half the height of the symbol. Clear space must stay free of graphics, text, other logos, or clutter.

### Minimum Size

| Logo | Print Minimum | Screen Minimum |
|---|---|---|
| Echo Primary Logo & Badge | 0.5" tall | 50 px tall |
| EchoDrive Logo & Badge | 0.7" tall | 70 px tall |
| EchoShip Logo & Badge | 0.7" tall | 70 px tall |
| EchoTrak Logo & Badge | 0.7" tall | 70 px tall |

When smaller sizing is needed, substitute the badge version (same minimums apply).

### Logo Don'ts
- Don't distort, stretch, or skew logo proportions.
- Don't rotate the wordmark or badge.
- Don't change the colors or outline of the wordmark or badge.
- Don't remove the Echo lines from the logo.
- Don't add effects (drop shadows, glows, gradients, etc.).
- Don't create stacked or otherwise edited versions of the badge or wordmark.

## 5. Brand Device

*[Full page: echo-brand-guidelines.webflow.io/brand-device]*

### Echo Range Device (Narrative)
Called "Range," the brand device is built from the outer shape of the "O" in the Echo wordmark, symbolizing Echo's reach across every corner of transportation management. It extends the equity of the logo into a flexible device usable across many contexts. [Download](https://www.echo.com/wp-content/themes/ws/brand-assets/Echo_Common_Range_Uses.zip)

### Range Applications
The Range device must never be stretched or altered outside approved parameters.

- **Fine Line Motif** — An elevated look using fine lines and layering for sophistication; usable on light and dark backgrounds. Use sparingly, as an accent only — never distracting or overwhelming.
- **Photo Layering** — Layering Range within photography adds depth; reserved for large spotlight placements like ads or website heroes.
- **Photo Framing** — Masking images to the Range device shape is a simple way to weave the brand into collateral.

Only use the approved motifs from the downloadable brand kit.

### Map Motif (Secondary Brand Pattern)
A custom pattern of fine lines resembling roads worldwide, symbolizing Echo's global presence and elevated customer experience. Use only to add texture to backgrounds — do not alter the provided maps.

## 9. Photography

*[Full page: echo-brand-guidelines.webflow.io/photography]*

### Photography Style
Imagery should reflect the company's tone and capture positive interactions/relationships between customers and the brand. Guidelines call for images that are unposed, rich, bright, and warm — not overly saturated or edited with flares. Subjects should be authentic, modern, candid, and diverse in gender, ethnicity, and age. Environmental shots should feel inviting and modern, highlighting subject matter relevant to Echo's audiences.

Photo Library: [Download](https://www.echo.com/wp-content/themes/ws/brand-assets/10-Photos.zip)

Categories referenced on the page: **Carriers**, **Shippers**, **Technology Employees** (image galleries — see live site).

### People
- **Do:** Choose inclusive imagery spanning a wide range of ages, genders, and ethnicities.
- **Don't:** Choose posed or inauthentic imagery — the goal is a connection to real people.

### Environment
- **Do:** Choose imagery reflecting audiences growing their companies.
- **Don't:** Choose imagery that's overly casual or inappropriate for the audience.

### Tone
- **Do:** Choose neutral-warm, rich-toned images.
- **Don't:** Choose overly saturated, cold images, or images with stylistic flourishes.
